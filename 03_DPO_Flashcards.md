# DPO Certification — Flashcards / Quick Recall

Cover the **A:** line and answer aloud. Cite the instrument where noted. (Act = Cyber and Data Protection Act [Chapter 12:07]; SI = SI 155 of 2024.)

---

## Foundations
**Q:** What is the primary data protection statute in Zimbabwe?
**A:** The Cyber and Data Protection Act [Chapter 12:07] (Act 5 of 2021).

**Q:** Who is the Data Protection Authority?
**A:** POTRAZ — Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe.

**Q:** What does SI 155 of 2024 regulate?
**A:** Licensing of data controllers and appointment of data protection officers.

**Q:** Under what section were the DPO guidelines issued?
**A:** Section 20(6) of the Act.

---

## Definitions
**Q:** Data controller?
**A:** A person who, alone or jointly, determines the *purposes and means* of processing personal data.

**Q:** Data processor?
**A:** A person who processes personal data on behalf of the controller.

**Q:** Data subject?
**A:** The identified/identifiable natural person the data relates to.

**Q:** Biometric data (SI definition + examples)?
**A:** Physiological characteristics related to a data subject — fingerprints, palm veins, face recognition.

**Q:** Sensitive/special personal data?
**A:** Racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious/philosophical beliefs, trade-union membership, health, sex life, biometric/genetic data, criminal history.

---

## The principles
**Q:** List the seven principles.
**A:** Lawfulness/fairness/transparency; purpose limitation; minimisation; accuracy; storage limitation; integrity & confidentiality (security); accountability.

**Q:** Which principle is breached by keeping data far longer than needed?
**A:** Storage limitation.

**Q:** Which principle makes the controller responsible for demonstrating compliance?
**A:** Accountability.

---

## Lawful bases & rights
**Q:** Name the six lawful bases.
**A:** Consent; contract; legal obligation; vital interests; public interest; legitimate interests.

**Q:** List the data subject rights.
**A:** Be informed; access; rectification; erasure; object; restrict; not be subject to solely automated decisions; complain to POTRAZ.

**Q:** Can a solely-automated decision with legal effects be made?
**A:** Only with the data subject's consent or where a law provides for it (SI s.10(3)).

---

## Licensing (SI ss.3–9)
**Q:** Which form applies for a data controller licence?
**A:** Form DP1.

**Q:** The four tiers by number of data subjects?
**A:** Tier 1: 50–1,000 · Tier 2: 1,001–100,000 · Tier 3: 100,001–500,000 · Tier 4: >500,000.

**Q:** Initial/renewal fees by tier?
**A:** T1 USD 50 · T2 USD 300 · T3 USD 500 · T4 USD 2,500 (application fee USD 30 for T2/3/4).

**Q:** How long is a licence valid, and when do you renew?
**A:** 12 months; renew at least 3 months before expiry.

**Q:** POTRAZ's decision window on an application?
**A:** 14 days.

**Q:** Three licensing exemptions?
**A:** Personal/family/household affairs; law enforcement; journalistic/historical/archival. (Last two must still register.)

---

## The DPO (SI ss.12–14)
**Q:** Which form notifies POTRAZ of a DPO appointment?
**A:** Form DP2.

**Q:** Deadline to appoint a DPO?
**A:** Within 90 days of promulgation or of the previous DPO's contract ending.

**Q:** Deadline to notify POTRAZ of a change/departure of DPO?
**A:** 14 days.

**Q:** DPO qualification routes (SI s.13)?
**A:** Data science; data analytics; information security systems; information systems audit; law; audit; or other relevant qualification — plus knowledge of data protection law and the controller's business.

**Q:** The six DPO functions (SI s.14)?
**A:** (a) Monitor compliance (audits, training, awareness); (b) handle POTRAZ & data-subject requests; (c) advise employees; (d) advise on/monitor DPIAs; (e) liaise with POTRAZ; (f) act as data-subject contact point.

**Q:** How often must a DPO recertify?
**A:** Annually — the controller must provide CPD training (SI s.10(1)).

**Q:** Who stays accountable for the DPO's and processors' acts?
**A:** The data controller (SI s.10(4)).

---

## Security & breaches (SI ss.16–17)
**Q:** Report a breach to POTRAZ within…?
**A:** 24 hours, on Form DP3.

**Q:** Notify affected data subjects of a high-risk breach within…?
**A:** 72 hours.

**Q:** Respond to a POTRAZ breach info request within…?
**A:** 14 days.

**Q:** Conclude the breach investigation and submit report within…?
**A:** 21 days of notification.

**Q:** What four security qualities must measures ensure?
**A:** Security, confidentiality, integrity and availability.

**Q:** What must controllers notify POTRAZ about (s.10(2))?
**A:** All processing activities; modifications to indirectly-collected data; cross-border transfers; biometric/genetic data processing.

---

## Penalties
**Q:** Standard penalty for most contraventions?
**A:** Fine up to level 11 and/or up to 7 years imprisonment.

**Q:** Penalty for failing to appoint a DPO?
**A:** Level 7 and/or up to 2 years — the notable exception (lower).

**Q:** Penalty for training without POTRAZ accreditation?
**A:** Level 11 and/or up to 7 years.

---

## Forms cheat-sheet
**Q:** DP1 / DP2 / DP3?
**A:** DP1 = controller licence application/renewal · DP2 = DPO appointment notification · DP3 = breach notification.
