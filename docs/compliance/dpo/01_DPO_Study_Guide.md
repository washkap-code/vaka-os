# Data Protection Officer (Zimbabwe) — Full Study Guide

**For the POTRAZ / HIT DPO Certification Course**
Based on the Cyber and Data Protection Act [Chapter 12:07] (Act 5 of 2021), Statutory Instrument 155 of 2024, and POTRAZ's DPO Implementation Guidelines.

> How to use this guide: read Part 1–3 for the law you must know verbatim, Part 4–6 for the DPO role and practice, and Part 7 for exam technique. The numbers, deadlines, forms and penalties in the boxes are the ones most likely to be tested — memorise them exactly.

---

## Part 1 — The legal landscape

### 1.1 The instruments
There are three documents that govern everything a Zimbabwean DPO does:

1. **Cyber and Data Protection Act [Chapter 12:07]** — the primary statute (Act 5 of 2021). It created data protection law in Zimbabwe, designated the Data Protection Authority, and set the principles and offences.
2. **SI 155 of 2024** — *Cyber and Data Protection (Licensing of Data Controllers and Appointment of Data Protection Officers) Regulations, 2024.* This is the operational rulebook: licensing tiers, fees, forms, timelines, DPO appointment and functions.
3. **POTRAZ Implementation Guidelines on the Appointment, Roles, Responsibilities, Training and Certification of DPOs** — issued under section 20(6) of the Act; repealed Regulatory Notice 1 of 2022.

### 1.2 Who is POTRAZ?
The **Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)** is the designated **Data Protection Authority (the "Authority")**. It licenses data controllers, accredits training, certifies DPOs, maintains the public register, investigates breaches and enforces penalties.

Also relevant: the **Cyber Security and Monitoring of Interception of Communications Centre** and the **Cyber Incident Response Team (zw-CIRT)**, which give technical security advice to controllers and processors.

## Part 2 — Key definitions (know these cold)

- **Personal data / personal information** — any information relating to an identified or identifiable data subject.
- **Data subject** — the identifiable natural person the data relates to.
- **Data controller** — a person who, alone or jointly, determines the *purposes and means* of processing personal data.
- **Data processor** — a person who processes data on behalf of the controller.
- **Processing** — any operation performed on personal data (collection, recording, storage, use, disclosure, erasure, etc.).
- **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** — the person appointed by a controller to monitor compliance and act as contact point.
- **Sensitive personal data** — data revealing racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious/philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, health, sex life, biometric or genetic data, criminal history, etc.
- **Biometric data** — physiological characteristics related to a data subject, including fingerprints, palm veins, and face recognition.
- **Consent** — freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous indication of the data subject's agreement.

## Part 3 — The data protection principles

Personal data must be:

1. **Lawful, fair and transparent** — processed on a valid legal basis, fairly, and openly.
2. **Purpose-limited** — collected for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes; not further processed incompatibly.
3. **Minimised** — adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary.
4. **Accurate** — kept accurate and up to date; inaccurate data erased or corrected.
5. **Storage-limited** — kept no longer than necessary for the purpose.
6. **Secure (integrity & confidentiality)** — protected by appropriate technical and organisational measures.
7. **Accountable** — the controller is responsible for, and must be able to demonstrate, compliance.

> Exam tip: expect a question asking you to list the principles and *apply* them to a scenario (e.g. a bank keeping loan records for 40 years fails **storage limitation**; a clinic emailing patient lists unencrypted fails **security**).

### 3.1 Lawful bases for processing
Processing is lawful where at least one applies: the data subject's **consent**; performance of a **contract**; compliance with a **legal obligation**; protection of **vital interests**; a task in the **public interest**; or the controller's **legitimate interests** (balanced against the data subject's rights). Sensitive data needs a stronger basis (explicit consent or specific legal authorisation).

### 3.2 Data subject rights
Right to be **informed**; right of **access**; right to **rectification**; right to **erasure**; right to **object**; right to **restrict** processing; right not to be subject to **solely automated decisions** that produce legal effects (without consent or legal basis); and the right to **lodge a complaint** with POTRAZ.

### 3.3 Children's data (SI 155, s.10(5))
- No processing of a child's data without **parent/guardian consent**.
- Make reasonable efforts to **verify** that consent.
- Apply all processing principles; conduct **regular DPIAs** for children's data.
- Build in **data protection by design and by default**.
- **No automated decision-making** that affects a child's rights.

## Part 4 — Licensing of data controllers (SI 155, ss.3–9)

### 4.1 Who must be licensed
Anyone who processes personal data and decides the means, purpose or outcome; decides what data to collect; decides which individuals to collect from; or obtains commercial gain from processing — **must apply for a data controller licence** using **Form DP1**.

### 4.2 Licence tiers (by number of data subjects)

| Tier | Data subjects | Initial/renewal fee |
|------|--------------|---------------------|
| Tier 1 | 50 – 1,000 | USD 50 |
| Tier 2 | 1,001 – 100,000 | USD 300 |
| Tier 3 | 100,001 – 500,000 | USD 500 |
| Tier 4 | more than 500,000 | USD 2,500 |

- **Application fee** for Tier 2/3/4: **USD 30**.
- Licence is valid **12 months**; renew with Form DP1 **at least 3 months before expiry**.
- POTRAZ must decide (approve / request more info / reject with reasons) **within 14 days**.
- Existing controllers had **6 months** from promulgation to apply.

### 4.3 Exemptions (s.8)
No licence needed where processing is for: **personal/family/household affairs**; **law enforcement**; or **journalistic, historical or archival purposes**. Note: law-enforcement and journalistic/historical/archival controllers must still **register** and comply with the principles.

## Part 5 — The Data Protection Officer

### 5.1 Appointment (SI 155, s.12)
- Every data controller **must appoint a DPO** and notify POTRAZ in writing using **Form DP2**.
- Appoint within **90 days** of promulgation or of a DPO contract ending.
- Notify POTRAZ of any change to the DPO's phone/email/address within **14 days**.
- Notify POTRAZ of a DPO's dismissal or resignation within **14 days**.
- **Penalty for failing to appoint a DPO:** fine not exceeding **level 7** or imprisonment up to **2 years**, or both. *(Note this is lower than the level-11 / 7-year penalties elsewhere.)*

### 5.2 Qualifications (SI 155, s.13)
A DPO must have skill, qualification or experience in one of: **data science; data analytics; information security systems; information systems audit; law; audit; or any other relevant qualification** — plus **knowledge of national data protection laws** and **understanding of the controller's business and processing activities**.

Every DPO must complete a **POTRAZ-approved certification course**, and only Authority-**accredited** trainers may provide it.

### 5.3 Functions (SI 155, s.14) — memorise the list
The DPO's duties are to:
- **(a) Monitor compliance** with the Act, regulations and internal policies — including managing internal data protection activities, **raising awareness**, **training staff**, and conducting **internal compliance audits**.
- **(b)** Handle requests from POTRAZ and from data subjects.
- **(c) Advise employees** on their obligations.
- **(d)** Advise on and **monitor DPIAs** (data protection impact assessments).
- **(e)** Work with **POTRAZ** on its functions relating to the controller.
- **(f)** Act as the **contact point** for data subjects about processing of their data.

### 5.4 Independence and support
The controller must provide **continuous professional development (CPD) training** to keep the DPO's certification current (s.10(1)), and the certification must be **renewed annually**. The DPO advises and monitors but the **controller remains accountable** — including for the acts of its DPO, processors and agents (s.10(4)).

## Part 6 — Security, breaches and transfers

### 6.1 Security of data (SI 155, s.16)
Process data securely using appropriate **technical and organisational measures**: risk assessments; organisational policies; physical and technical controls across all data phases; ensuring **security, confidentiality, integrity and availability**; ability to **restore** data after an incident; and **testing** the effectiveness of measures. zw-CIRT may advise.

### 6.2 Breach notification (SI 155, s.17) — heavily tested timelines
- Report a breach to **POTRAZ within 24 hours** of becoming aware, using **Form DP3**.
- If the breach is likely to be **high risk** to individuals' rights, inform the **affected data subjects within 72 hours**.
- Keep a **record of all breaches**; maintain detection, investigation and internal reporting procedures.
- Respond to POTRAZ information requests within **14 days**; conclude the investigation and submit a report within **21 days** of notification.

### 6.3 Notifications to POTRAZ (s.10(2))
Controllers must notify POTRAZ of: all processing activities; modifications to indirectly-collected data; any **cross-border transfer** of data outside Zimbabwe; and any processing of **biometric or genetic** data.

### 6.4 Automated decisions (s.10(3))
No decision based *solely* on automated processing that produces legal effects — unless the data subject **consents** or a **law** provides for it.

### 6.5 Codes of conduct (s.15)
Filed with POTRAZ for approval under section 30 of the Act. POTRAZ checks Act-compliance, representation level, purpose statement, covered processing, monitoring methods, and consistency with other law. POTRAZ maintains a register of approved codes.

## Part 7 — Offences and penalties (memorise the levels)

| Contravention | Penalty |
|---------------|---------|
| Processing without a data controller licence | Fine up to **level 11** and/or up to **7 years** imprisonment |
| Failing to renew a licence on time | Level 11 and/or up to 7 years |
| Submitting false information on application | Level 11 and/or up to 7 years |
| Breaching controller obligations (s.10) | Level 11 and/or up to 7 years |
| Providing certification training without accreditation | Level 11 and/or up to 7 years |
| Breach of security of data (s.16) | Level 11 and/or up to 7 years |
| Breach-notification failures (s.17) | Level 11 and/or up to 7 years |
| **Failing to appoint a DPO (s.12)** | **Level 7 and/or up to 2 years** |

> Exam tip: the odd-one-out is **failure to appoint a DPO = level 7 / 2 years**. Almost everything else is **level 11 / 7 years**. Questions love to test this distinction.

## Part 8 — The forms (know what each is for)

- **Form DP1** — Data Controller Application / Renewal (First Schedule). Captures tier, business type, sensitive data handled, data location, security safeguards, designated DPO, and a sworn declaration.
- **Form DP2** — DPO Designation / Appointment Notification (Third Schedule). Captures controller details, licence number, DPO name, DPO registration number, contacts and DPO qualifications.
- **Form DP3** — Breach Notification (Fourth Schedule). Captures breach dates, systems breached, nature/volume of records affected, likely impact, and remedial measures.

## Part 9 — Exam technique

- **The exam is one 4-hour written paper.** Expect a mix of definitions, short-answer law, and applied scenarios.
- **Cite the source.** When you state a rule, name the instrument and section (e.g. "SI 155, s.17(1)"). Graders reward precision.
- **Nail the numbers.** 24 hours (breach to POTRAZ), 72 hours (to data subjects), 14 days (POTRAZ licence decision / DPO detail changes), 21 days (breach report), 90 days (DPO appointment), 3 months (renewal lead time), 6 months (existing-controller licensing), 12 months (licence validity), annual DPO recertification.
- **Learn the four tiers and their fees** as a table — they are easy marks.
- **Practise applying principles to facts.** Most scenario questions want you to (1) name the principle/right/section breached, (2) explain why, (3) state the fix, (4) state the penalty exposure.
- **Structure long answers** with headings/lists; state the rule, apply it, conclude.
