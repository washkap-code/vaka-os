# DPO Certification — Application Checklist (HIT / POTRAZ)

The official DPO certification in Zimbabwe is run by **Harare Institute of Technology (HIT)** as POTRAZ's appointed National Data Protection Training Institution. There is **no private/alternative route** to the recognised certificate — by law (SI 155, s.11 & s.13) only a POTRAZ-accredited institution may certify DPOs. Verify current dates and fees with HIT before paying, as intakes and figures can change.

---

## Step 1 — Confirm you're eligible
You should have a background (skill, qualification or experience) in at least one of:
- Data science / data analytics
- Information security systems / information systems audit
- Law / audit
- Any other relevant qualification

Plus a willingness to learn national data protection law and your organisation's processing operations. (SI 155, s.13.)

- [ ] I meet at least one qualification route above.

- [ ] Download the **DPO Certification Course Application Form** from the HIT website (Short Courses / Events section: `hit.ac.zw`).
- [ ] Alternatively request it by emailing **dpotraining@hit.ac.zw**.

## Step 4 — Submit the application
- [ ] Submit the completed form + proof of payment, physically at HIT campus or by email to **dpotraining@hit.ac.zw**.
- [ ] Keep a copy of everything you send.

---

## Step 5 — On approval, pay tuition
- **Zimbabwean citizens:** USD 1,250
- **International applicants:** USD 1,450
- [ ] Tuition paid once the application is approved; proof saved.
- [ ] Confirm intake dates and mode (in-person at HIT, Belvedere, Harare / online) with the course office.

---

## Step 6 — Attend and complete the course
- [ ] Attend all sessions.
- [ ] Cover the syllabus (see Study Guide): the Act, SI 155, principles, rights, licensing, DPO role, security, breaches, DPIAs.

---

## Step 7 — Sit the examination
- [ ] Sit the **4-hour written exam** at the end of the course.
- [ ] Pass to be issued the **Data Protection Certificate**.

---

## Step 8 — After certification
- [ ] If you're being appointed as a DPO for an organisation, the controller notifies POTRAZ using **Form DP2** (within 90 days of appointment).
- [ ] Diarise **annual recertification** — the certificate must be renewed each year, with CPD training provided by your employer (SI 155, s.10(1)).

### On training privately — the direct answer
No. POTRAZ will not train you one-on-one, and no private trainer can issue the recognised DPO certificate. Under SI 155 anyone providing certification training must be **accredited by POTRAZ** (accreditation fee USD 5,000 p.a.), and providing certification training without accreditation is an offence (level 11 / up to 7 years). Consultancies may offer *preparatory* workshops, but the statutory certification and exam must go through HIT/POTRAZ. Your private effort is best spent on self-study (this pack) before and during the official course.
