# VAKA Voice and Tone

**Status:** Foundational direction
**Owner:** Brand and Product
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-04

## 1. Voice

VAKA sounds like a capable business partner: professional, calm, concise, executive, respectful, and highly intelligent.

The voice is consistent across marketing, product interfaces, support, documentation, reports, and VAKA AI. Tone may change with context, but the underlying character does not.

## 2. Voice principles

### Professional

Use clear business language and complete thoughts. Treat every user as a capable professional.

### Calm

Explain urgency without drama. Errors, overdue balances, stock shortages, and security events should be direct and composed.

### Concise

Say what matters, provide the necessary context, and end with a useful next step.

### Executive

Lead with the outcome, implication, or decision. Do not bury the important point beneath system detail.

### Respectful

Never patronise users, shame them, or make assumptions based on business size, language, location, or technical confidence.

### Highly intelligent

Show intelligence through relevance, accuracy, prioritisation, and judgment—not jargon or verbosity.

## 3. Writing rules

Use:

- short, direct sentences;
- active voice;
- outcome-led headings;
- concrete nouns and verbs;
- familiar business terminology;
- plain explanations of technical ideas;
- specific dates, currencies, and quantities where relevant; and
- one clear next action.

Avoid:

- generic ERP language;
- unnecessary technical jargon;
- long introductions;
- excessive exclamation marks;
- excessive uppercase copy;
- empty enthusiasm;
- vague promises;
- inflated AI claims; and
- unsupported words such as “revolutionary,” “game-changing,” “cutting-edge,” or “seamless.”

## 4. Tone by situation

| Situation | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing | Confident and concise | “Run customers, sales, finance and inventory from one connected system.” |
| Onboarding | Clear and reassuring | “Start with your company details. You can update these settings later.” |
| Success | Brief and factual | “Invoice INV-00124 was issued.” |
| Warning | Calm and specific | “Four products are below their reorder levels.” |
| Error | Direct and recoverable | “The payment was not recorded. Check the amount and try again.” |
| Permission denied | Respectful and secure | “You do not have permission to issue invoices.” |
| Destructive action | Explicit | “Voiding this invoice creates reversing entries. The original record remains in the audit trail.” |
| Security | Serious without panic | “We ended this session because the token expired. Sign in again to continue.” |
| Billing | Firm and humane | “Your account is read-only while the balance is overdue. Your data remains available for export.” |
| Support | Attentive and practical | “Send us the invoice number and the time the error occurred.” |

## 5. Outcome-led language

Lead with what the customer can accomplish.

Prefer:

- **Win more customers**
- **Get paid faster**
- **Never lose track of stock**
- **Stay compliant**
- **Make better decisions**
- **Let VAKA AI assist your business**

Avoid leading with internal feature names such as:

- contact management module;
- accounts receivable functionality;
- inventory database;
- reporting engine; or
- AI-powered analytics feature.

Module names may follow the outcome as supporting context.

## 6. VAKA AI voice

VAKA AI must sound:

- professional;
- calm;
- concise;
- executive;
- respectful; and
- highly intelligent.

It must not sound:

- childish;
- robotic;
- verbose;
- overly excited;
- patronising;
- falsely certain; or
- casually conversational about serious business matters.

Avoid phrases such as:

- “Awesome!”
- “Great question!”
- “I’d be happy to help!”
- “No worries!”
- excessive emojis; and
- generic filler before the answer.

### VAKA AI response structure

Where useful, responses should follow:

1. **Answer:** what is happening.
2. **Evidence:** the business data supporting the answer.
3. **Implication:** why it matters.
4. **Recommendation:** the next best action.
5. **Uncertainty:** missing information or limits.

Example:

> Three invoices are overdue, totalling US$6,240. Two belong to customers who usually pay within seven days. Following up with those customers today would address 81% of the overdue balance.

VAKA AI must distinguish facts, calculations, inferences, and recommendations. It must never invent data or imply that an action was completed when it was not.

## 7. Brand terminology

Use consistently:

- VAKA
- VAKA OS
- The Operating System for African Business.
- Designed in Zimbabwe. Built for Africa.
- ChiShona
- isiNdebele
- USD
- ZiG in customer-facing Zimbabwean copy, with technical currency codes handled separately

Use “customer” and “business” in marketing copy unless a more precise term is required. Avoid switching casually between customer, client, account, organisation, company, and tenant.

“Tenant” is an internal technical term and should rarely appear in customer-facing copy.

## 8. Localisation

English, ChiShona, and isiNdebele must preserve the same dignity, clarity, and business meaning.

- Do not translate word for word when natural phrasing differs.
- Use native or professionally qualified reviewers.
- Review financial, payroll, legal, tax, security, and AI terminology carefully.
- Do not infer a person’s preferred language from their name or location.
- Keep machine values and permission behavior independent from translated labels.
- Never publish machine-generated translations as final production copy without human review.

## 9. Microcopy checklist

Before publishing, ask:

1. Is the main point visible immediately?
2. Is the business outcome clear?
3. Is every claim accurate?
4. Does the user know what to do next?
5. Is the tone calm and respectful?
6. Can unnecessary words be removed?
7. Does translated text preserve the same meaning?
8. Would VAKA AI sound credible saying this aloud?
