# VAKA Brand Book

**Status:** Foundational direction
**Owner:** VAKA / Jonomi Digital
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-04

## 1. Brand foundation

### Master brand

**VAKA**

### Product name

**VAKA OS**

### Primary tagline

> **The Operating System for African Business.**

### Positioning line

> **Designed in Zimbabwe. Built for Africa.**

### Name and meaning

VAKA means **“build” in Shona**, derived from the verb *kuvaka*.

The name reflects what business owners do every day: build a customer base, a team, a reputation, a livelihood, and a legacy. VAKA exists to give African businesses stronger infrastructure for that work.

## 2. Brand idea

VAKA is a connected operating system through which an African business can run, understand, and improve its operations.

It is not merely a CRM, accounting package, inventory application, or collection of dashboards. VAKA brings customers, sales, finance, stock, people, operations, and intelligence into one trusted system.

Zimbabwe is the first launch market and the source of VAKA’s earliest product insight. It is not the limit of the brand.

## 3. Brand promise

VAKA gives African businesses a clearer, safer, and more connected way to run.

We earn that promise through:

- useful business outcomes;
- connected records and workflows;
- accurate financial and stock information;
- visible permissions and auditability;
- responsible localisation;
- reliable data ownership and export; and
- practical, permission-aware intelligence.

## 4. Brand feeling

Every VAKA experience should feel:

- **Premium:** considered, polished, and worth trusting with important work.
- **African:** grounded in real African business conditions without stereotypes.
- **Modern:** current, capable, and designed for how businesses work now.
- **Intelligent:** clear thinking made visible, without unnecessary complexity.
- **Trustworthy:** accurate, transparent, secure, and dependable.
- **Enterprise-grade:** disciplined enough for serious organisations and controls.
- **Simple:** understandable at first use, even when the underlying system is powerful.

## 5. Brand character

VAKA is:

- assured, not arrogant;
- ambitious, not inflated;
- warm, not casual about serious work;
- precise, not cold;
- African, not stereotyped;
- simple, not simplistic;
- intelligent, not showy; and
- executive, not bureaucratic.

## 6. Audience

VAKA primarily serves:

- Zimbabwean SME owners;
- founders and owner-managers;
- growing companies moving beyond spreadsheets and disconnected applications;
- finance, sales, inventory, people, and operations teams;
- diaspora owners who need reliable remote visibility; and
- partners and investors assessing a Zimbabwe-first, Africa-scalable platform.

Communications must respect the audience’s capability. Never patronise users or imply that African businesses need simplified imitations of global products.

## 7. Positioning

For African businesses that need greater control over customers, money, stock, people, and daily operations, VAKA is a trusted business operating system that connects essential workflows and turns business activity into clear action.

Unlike fragmented tools and generic imported software, VAKA is designed in Zimbabwe around local operating realities and built to localise responsibly across African markets.

## 8. Message hierarchy

Use the following order:

1. **VAKA**
2. **The Operating System for African Business.**
3. **Designed in Zimbabwe. Built for Africa.**
4. The practical outcome for the audience
5. The connected product capabilities
6. The meaning: **VAKA means build**

Do not attempt to communicate every module before the visitor understands the category and value.

## 9. Visual principles

### Premium, not flashy

Use restraint, hierarchy, whitespace, and confident composition. Avoid decorative effects that make serious business software feel temporary.

### Product-led

Show real or approved representative VAKA experiences. Demonstrate connected workflows rather than filling pages with abstract SaaS graphics.

### African, not clichéd

African identity should emerge through:

- the VAKA story;
- language;
- real customer needs;
- local product capability;
- Zimbabwe-first localisation;
- confident storytelling; and
- approved photography of real people and businesses.

Do not use generic maps of Africa, safari imagery, acacia trees, tribal-pattern decoration, or other shorthand for “African.”

### Spacious and precise

Use strong typography, generous whitespace, controlled colour, and fewer words. Important content should feel easy to find.

## 10. Avoid

Do not use:

- generic ERP language;
- cheap startup design conventions;
- African clichés;
- cluttered dashboards;
- overused stock photography;
- unsupported superlatives;
- fake customer evidence;
- inflated AI claims;
- excessive gradients, glow, glassmorphism, or decorative motion;
- jargon where direct business language will do; or
- “all-in-one” without explaining how the system is connected.

## 11. Product naming

Use:

- **VAKA** for the master brand;
- **VAKA OS** for the platform;
- **VAKA CRM**;
- **VAKA Finance**;
- **VAKA Inventory**;
- **VAKA Payroll**; and
- **VAKA AI**.

Only present a module as available when it is implemented and tested. Use **Coming Soon**, **Preview**, or **Early Access** consistently for future capabilities.

## 12. Trust and accuracy

Trust, data protection, permissions, auditability, reliability, and ownership are part of the brand.

Therefore:

- distinguish live capabilities from product direction;
- never invent customer logos, testimonials, ratings, usage figures, or business results;
- never imply regulatory approval that has not been obtained;
- do not claim integrations, payroll automation, AI actions, or language support before verification;
- use synthetic or approved demo data in marketing visuals; and
- keep legal, accounting, tax, security, and localisation claims subject to qualified review.

## 13. Core expressions

Approved expressions include:

- **The Operating System for African Business.**
- **Designed in Zimbabwe. Built for Africa.**
- **VAKA means build.**
- **Build Better Business.**
- **We built VAKA so you can build yours.**
- **One business. One system. One source of truth.**

Use them deliberately rather than repeating all of them on every surface.

## 14. Application checklist

Before approving brand work, ask:

1. Does it make VAKA feel premium and trustworthy?
2. Is the customer outcome clear?
3. Does it feel designed in Zimbabwe without relying on cliché?
4. Is the information calm, spacious, and understandable?
5. Are live and future capabilities distinguished?
6. Does it support English, ChiShona, and isiNdebele?
7. Is it usable and accessible on mobile?
8. Would a serious business trust this experience?
