# VAKA Homepage Specification

**Version:** 2.0
**Status:** Approved product direction
**Owner:** VAKA / Jonomi Digital
**Launch market:** Zimbabwe
**Expansion vision:** Africa
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-04

## 1. Purpose

The VAKA homepage must establish VAKA as:

> **The Operating System for African Business.**

Within five seconds, a visitor should understand:

1. VAKA helps run a whole business.
2. It is designed in Zimbabwe and built for African business realities.
3. It connects customers, sales, accounting, inventory, payroll, operations, and intelligence.
4. The product is practical, credible, and simple to begin.
5. Live and upcoming capabilities are labelled honestly.

The page must sell a vision and a practical product at the same time.

## 3. Brand and design direction

The homepage should feel:

- premium;
- African;
- modern;
- intelligent;
- trustworthy;
- enterprise-grade; and
- simple.

Avoid:

- generic ERP language;
- cheap startup design;
- African clichés;
- cluttered dashboards;
- overused stock photos;
- excessive gradients, glow, glassmorphism, or decorative motion;
- generic abstract SaaS illustrations; and
- unsupported claims.

African identity should come from the VAKA story, language, local product capability, real businesses, and confident Zimbabwe-first storytelling.

## 4. Audience

The homepage must speak to:

- Zimbabwean SME owners;
- founders and family businesses;
- growing companies moving beyond spreadsheets;
- finance, sales, inventory, payroll, and operations teams;
- diaspora owners who need remote visibility; and
- partners and investors evaluating a Zimbabwe-first, Africa-scalable platform.

## 5. Accuracy rules

The homepage may communicate VAKA’s platform vision, but it must distinguish live functionality from future capability.

Do not present the following as live unless implemented and tested:

- payroll and statutory automation;
- VAKA AI actions;
- ChiShona or isiNdebele interfaces;
- ZIMRA integration or fiscalisation;
- PAYE or NSSA automation;
- bank integrations;
- offline mode;
- payment gateways; or
- market-specific functionality outside Zimbabwe.

Use clear labels such as:

- **Available now**
- **Coming soon**
- **Preview**
- **Early access**

Do not invent customer logos, testimonials, user counts, transaction volumes, ratings, or results.

## 6. Homepage structure

The homepage must use this primary structure:

1. Hero
2. Why VAKA exists
3. Built first for Zimbabwe
4. Business outcomes
5. Product modules
6. VAKA AI
7. Trust and security
8. Pricing
9. FAQ
10. Final CTA

Global navigation and the footer sit outside this numbered story.

The page should feel like one connected argument rather than ten unrelated sections.

## 11. Section 4 — Business outcomes

Lead with outcomes, not feature lists.

Required outcomes:

### Win more customers

Keep every lead, conversation, and opportunity organised.

### Get paid faster

Create invoices, track payments, and know what is overdue.

### Never lose track of stock

Know what is available, what is moving, and what needs attention.

### Stay compliant

Maintain reliable records, permissions, and audit history. Do not imply regulatory certification or automated statutory filing unless verified.

### Make better decisions

Turn connected business activity into information management can act on.

### Let VAKA AI assist your business

Ask questions, understand priorities, and receive grounded recommendations. Label this as preview or coming soon until live.

Each outcome should be paired with a real or approved representative VAKA interface fragment.

## 12. Section 5 — Product modules

Present modules as one connected operating system:

| Module | Customer outcome | Availability requirement |
|---|---|---|
| VAKA CRM | Organise customers and opportunities | Use live product evidence |
| VAKA Finance | Invoice, record payments, and understand financial position | Use live product evidence |
| VAKA Inventory | Track products, warehouses, purchasing, and stock movement | Use live product evidence |
| VAKA Payroll | Pay people accurately and manage statutory obligations | Mark coming soon until implemented and professionally reviewed |
| VAKA Operations | Coordinate connected business work | Define scope before presenting as a standalone live module |
| VAKA AI | Understand the business and prioritise action | Mark preview or coming soon until production-ready |

Show how data moves between modules. Avoid module grids that make VAKA look like unrelated applications.

## 13. Section 6 — VAKA AI

### Heading

**Your business can finally talk back.**

### Supporting copy

Ask questions in plain language. Understand what is happening. Know what needs your attention. Take action faster.

### Tone

VAKA AI must sound:

- professional;
- calm;
- concise;
- executive;
- respectful; and
- highly intelligent.

It must not sound childish, robotic, verbose, overly excited, patronising, or falsely certain.

### Interaction model

**Ask → Understand → Recommend → Act**

The homepage must not claim that VAKA AI can execute actions until permissions, confirmations, audits, evaluation, and production safeguards exist.

### Example

**User:** Which customers need my attention today?

**VAKA AI:** Three invoices are overdue, totalling US$6,240. Two belong to customers who usually pay within seven days. Following up with those customers today would address 81% of the overdue balance.

If AI is not live, label the experience:

> **VAKA AI Preview — coming soon**

## 15. Section 8 — Pricing

### Heading

**Start where you are. Grow without changing systems.**

Plans should identify the customer:

- **Starter:** founders and very small businesses
- **Growth:** growing teams
- **Business:** established companies
- **Enterprise:** larger and more complex organisations

Plan outcomes, limits, live/planned labels, add-ons, and entitlements must
follow `PRICING-AND-PACKAGING.md`. The homepage must not describe a planned
capability as included and live merely because its future tier is known.
Recommended revised prices remain unpublished until leadership validation,
cost/margin review, entitlement enforcement, billing migration, and an explicit
go-live decision.

Each plan should show:

- price;
- billing period;
- user allowance;
- primary outcome;
- included live modules;
- upcoming modules clearly marked; and
- CTA.

Use one consistent proposition for free workspace, trial, demo, and early access.

Pricing displayed on the homepage must share a governed source of truth with platform billing.

## 19. Localisation

The homepage must be localisation-ready for:

- English;
- ChiShona; and
- isiNdebele.

Requirements:

- all visible copy stored in typed localisation catalogues;
- persistent language selection;
- browser-language awareness;
- verified English fallback;
- translated metadata;
- translated navigation;
- translated accessibility labels;
- translated validation and service messages;
- text-expansion testing; and
- native-speaker review.

Do not use machine-generated translations as final production copy without qualified human review.

## 20. Responsive behavior

Design intentionally for:

- mobile;
- tablet;
- laptop;
- desktop; and
- large displays.

Mobile is not a compressed desktop page.

On mobile:

- simplify product compositions;
- reduce simultaneous motion;
- keep CTA access obvious;
- preserve readable type;
- stack connected workflows intelligently;
- prevent horizontal overflow; and
- maintain accessible touch targets.

## 21. Accessibility

Minimum target: **WCAG 2.2 AA**.

Requirements include:

- semantic HTML;
- keyboard navigation;
- visible focus;
- sufficient contrast;
- accessible names;
- correct heading hierarchy;
- reduced-motion support;
- labelled forms;
- descriptive alt text; and
- no colour-only meaning.

## 22. Performance

Target excellent Core Web Vitals.

- minimise layout shift;
- optimise and size media;
- lazy-load non-critical assets;
- use responsive formats;
- avoid unnecessary JavaScript;
- keep motion performant; and
- remain usable before decorative content loads.

## 23. SEO

Target natural themes including:

- business software Zimbabwe;
- accounting software Zimbabwe;
- CRM Zimbabwe;
- inventory software Zimbabwe;
- payroll software Zimbabwe;
- African business software; and
- business operating system.

Include:

- unique title;

Do not target payroll search terms in a way that implies payroll is live before it is available.

## 24. Analytics

Subject to VAKA’s approved privacy framework, measure:

- primary CTA clicks;
- demo and early-access intent;
- signup starts and completions;
- pricing views;
- module interactions;
- VAKA AI preview engagement;
- language changes;
- FAQ engagement;
- scroll depth; and
- form abandonment.

Do not collect unnecessary personal data.

## 25. Required states

Provide:

- loading state;
- media fallback;
- animation fallback;
- localisation fallback;
- JavaScript-reduced usability;
- form success and error states;
- unavailable-service messaging; and
- graceful behavior when optional media or APIs fail.

## 26. Success criteria

A first-time visitor should be able to answer within 30 seconds:

1. What is VAKA?
2. Who is it for?
3. Why is it different?
4. Why was it designed in Zimbabwe?
5. What business outcomes does it support?
6. Which modules are live and which are coming?
7. What is VAKA AI?
8. Can VAKA be trusted?
9. How do I begin?

The page should create:

- **Recognition:** “This understands my business reality.”
- **Confidence:** “This looks credible enough to run my business.”
- **Ambition:** “This is where African business software is going.”

## 27. Implementation rules

Before implementation:

1. Read `AGENTS.md`.
2. Read the VAKA Constitution.
3. Read Product Philosophy.
4. Read Brand Positioning.
5. Read the Codebase Audit.
6. Read the Homepage Gap Analysis.
7. Inspect the current homepage.
8. Identify reusable components.
9. Preserve working authentication and application workflows.
10. Create regression tests before visual or functional changes.

Do not perform a blind rewrite.

Separate:

- visual changes;
- copy changes;
- localisation changes;
- analytics changes;
- backend changes; and
- AI changes.

Implementation must preserve existing routes, backend contracts, tenant data, accounting and inventory integrity, and working authentication unless a separately approved migration changes them.
